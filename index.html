Final Verdict & Recommendation

Recommended: Longstanton (Mills Lane) — with a caveat

Longstanton scores higher overall thanks to its stronger appreciation potential (Cambridge proximity), lower all-in cost (~£655k vs ~£735k), better energy efficiency (EPC C), excellent nursery and all-through schooling, no onward chain, and cheaper season tickets. It's a more future-proof investment in a rapidly developing area.

However, the caveat is your wife's commute. Alconbury keeps her on the Thameslink line she already knows from St Neots, giving her a ~1h 25min door-to-door journey to Farringdon. From Longstanton, her commute becomes ~1h 50min with a bus-to-train-to-tube journey. Since she commutes 3 times a week, this 25-minute-each-way difference (over 150 extra minutes per week) is significant. You should discuss this trade-off carefully as a family.

If your wife's commute is the dealbreaker: Alconbury is the safer choice for day-to-day quality of life. The 0.66-acre garden is genuinely exceptional, and if the planned Alconbury Weald railway station materialises, property values could see a meaningful uplift.
